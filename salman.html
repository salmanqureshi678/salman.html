Babar made a huge impact during his time at the Cooper Associates County Ground in 2019. The 25-year-old was the leading run scorer in the 2019 Vitality Blast, amassing an incredible 578 runs at an average of 52.54. He struck a sublime 102 not out at the Ageas Bowl and boasted an impressive tournament strike rate of 149.35. His prowess with the bat saw him score one century and four half centuries from his 13 innings. He also struck 60 fours and 14 sixes during the competition.

  • The country I grew up in has always been dear to me. Pakistan is a Muslim republic that is situated in South Asia. The modern, multi-ethnic state of Pakistan was founded in 1947. The struggle for Pakistan was started when the Muslims of the sub-continent got harassed by the Indian government and were not getting their proper rights. Pakistani leader Sir Syed Ahmed Khan firstly used the word“Nation” for the Muslims. After his “Two Nation Theory” and Allama Iqbal's thought about a separate state in which Muslims can live according to Islamic teachings. The government is home to 28% of the world's total Hindu population and 20% of the Muslims. Pakistan is an Asian country and a federal republic. It is located in South Asia. As well as Islamabad, Pakistan has its most prominent city Karachi. It was established as a region on October 2nd, 1947, from the separation of India. Pakistan has a population of more than 200 million.

  • The country is 96% Muslim and has a diversified economy, including agriculture, textiles, food processing, pharmaceuticals, and manufacturing. With over 100 million speakers, Urdu is Pakistan's official language. It's spoken not only in the country but also in neighboring countries. Punjabi is the second most prevalent language, with 45% of the population saying it. Language plays a significant role in the culture and identity of Pakistan. For example, the national dress includes Shalwar Kameez, which is worn as an outer garment or casual. Pakistan's lowlands have fertile soil, and the Indus Valley makes for excellent agriculture. The land is elaborately productive; it grows everything from cotton to wheat to rice. Pakistan's fertile land and temperate weather make it an ideal place to grow crops containing high concentrations of nutrients. Pakistani cities are also home to major industrial manufacturing plants that are owned and operated by multinational corporations
